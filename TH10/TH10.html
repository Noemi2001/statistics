Thesis 10: The Wiener process and the GBM, Derivations and simulations

The Wiener process, also known as Brownian motion, is a continuous-time stochastic process that has independent and normally distributed increments. The most important properties of the Wiener process are:

  1. Continuous Paths: Brownian motion has continuous sample paths.
  2. Gaussian Increments: The increments of the process over disjoint time intervals are independent and normally distributed.
Brownian motion can be also considered as the generalization of a Randomwalk with jump according to the normal distribution. So, it is a scaling limit of the Randomwalk for n->infinity.

The differential form of the Wiener process is often written as: \[ dW(t) = \mu \, dt + \sigma \, dZ(t) \] where:
- \(W(t)\) is the value of the Wiener process at time \(t\),
- \(\mu\) is the drift parameter,
- \(\sigma\) is the volatility (= diffusion parameter),
- \(Z(t)\) is a standard Brownian motion,
- \(dt\) is an infinitesimally small time increment.

The Geometric Brownian Motion is a stochastic process that is often used to model the stock prices in financial mathematics. It is an extension of the Wiener process with a drift and a volatility term. The differential equation for GBM is given by: \[ dS(t) = \mu S(t) \, dt + \sigma S(t) \, dZ(t) \] where:
- \(S(t)\) is the stock price at time \(t\),
- \(\mu\) is the drift (average rate of return),
- \(\sigma\) is the volatility (standard deviation of the rate of return),
- \(Z(t)\) is a standard Brownian motion,
- \(dt\) is an infinitesimally small time increment.

Applications:

The Wiener process (Brownian motion) and the Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) have various applications in different fields, including finance, physics, and biology. Here are some notable applications:
  1. Finance: Stock Price Modeling, Option Pricing and Risk Management.
  2. Physics: Diffusion Processes of molecules.
  3. Biology: Population Dynamics
  4. Computer Science: Monte Carlo Simulations
  5. Engineering: Signal Processing
  6. Climate Modeling: Climate Modeling
